UPI and online payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India today. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or by approving fake payment requests.

A typical fraud begins when a victim receives a call, message, or link claiming to be from a bank, delivery service, or job provider. The attacker may ask the victim to scan a QR code or click a link to “receive money.” Once the victim enters their UPI PIN or approves the request, money is instantly deducted from their account. In some cases, fake apps or screen-sharing apps are also used to gain access.

College students and new digital users are often targeted because they may not fully understand how UPI transactions work. People expecting payments (like selling items online) are especially vulnerable.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes misuse of personal data. Recovering money can be difficult if not reported quickly.

This highlights the importance of awareness, as simple mistakes can lead to serious financial damage.
